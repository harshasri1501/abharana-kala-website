About the Research

Ancient India valued artistic expression as an important part of culture, spirituality, and education. Jewelry design and metal craftsmanship were deeply connected with the traditional 64 Kalās described in ancient Indian texts.

These arts included ornament design, gem setting, carving, decorative design, engraving, and fine craftsmanship. Indian ornaments represented beauty, prosperity, status, and cultural identity.

Kalās Associated with Jewelry Design

Ābharaṇa Yojanam

Art of designing and arranging ornaments and jewelry. 2 It focuses on decorative accessories and personal adornment.

Maṇi Bhūmikā Karma

Art of gemstone setting and precious stone craftsmanship. This Kalā is connected to luxury jewelry creation.

Citrayoga / Alekhyam

Decorative design and artistic sketching used in jewelry patterns and ornament layouts.

Takṣaṇa

Art of carving and shaping materials used for metal engraving and ornament crafting.

Rūpya-Ratna Parīkṣā

Knowledge of silver and precious stones used for examining jewelry quality and purity.

Sūci Vāpa Karma

Fine decorative handcrafting and beadwork techniques used in ornamental detailing.

Cultural Significance

Jewelry and metal crafts in India were not only decorative arts but also symbols of spirituality, prosperity, social identity, and heritage.

Ancient artisans combined creativity with technical precision to create ornaments that reflected India’s rich artistic traditions.
