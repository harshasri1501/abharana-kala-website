64 KALĀS OF ANCIENT INDIA

The 64 Kalās were traditional Indian arts and skills described in ancient Indian literature. These included music, painting, architecture, ornament design, sculpture, decorative arts, storytelling, and craftsmanship.

Ābharaṇa Yojanam

Art of ornament planning and jewelry arrangement. Traditional Indian jewelry represented beauty, spirituality, and status.

Maṇi Bhūmikā Karma

Art of gemstone setting and jewelry craftsmanship using precious stones and metals.

Citrayoga / Alekhyam

Artistic sketching and decorative jewelry design patterns used in ornament creation.

Takṣaṇa

Metal carving, engraving, shaping, and decorative craftsmanship techniques.

Rūpya-Ratna Parīkṣā

Knowledge of silver, gemstones, and diamond examination for quality and purity.
