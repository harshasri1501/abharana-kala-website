64 KALĀS OF ANCIENT INDIA

The 64 Kalās were traditional Indian arts and skills described in ancient Indian literature. These included music, painting, architecture, ornament design, sculpture, decorative arts, storytelling, and craftsmanship.

Ābharaṇa Yojanam (आभरण योजनम्)

Art of ornament planning and jewelry arrangement. Traditional Indian jewelry represented beauty, spirituality, elegance, and royal identity.

Maṇi Bhūmikā Karma

Traditional gemstone setting and precious jewelry craftsmanship. This Kalā focused on luxury ornament creation using precious metals and stones.

Citrayoga / Alekhyam (चित्रयोग / आलेख्यम्)

Decorative sketching and jewelry illustration used for ornament pattern creation, creative design layouts, and artistic planning.

Takṣaṇa (तक्षण)

Art of metal engraving, carving, shaping, and decorative craftsmanship in traditional jewelry work.

Rūpya-Ratna Parīkṣā

Knowledge of diamonds, silver, gemstones, and quality examination techniques in ancient Indian ornament traditions.
