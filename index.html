64 KALĀS OF ANCIENT INDIA

The 64 Kalās were traditional Indian arts and skills described in ancient Indian literature. These Kalās represented creativity, craftsmanship, artistic excellence, culture, decorative arts, architecture, music, painting, and ornament creation.

Ābharaṇa Kalā is one among these classical traditions, focusing on jewelry design, gemstone arrangement, ornamental decoration, engraving, and artistic craftsmanship.

ART OF ORNAMENT DESIGN

Ābharaṇa Yojanam (आभरण योजनम्)

Ābharaṇa Yojanam refers to the ancient Indian art of designing, planning, and arranging ornaments and jewelry. This Kalā focused on elegance, artistic balance, symmetry, and decorative beauty.

Traditional Indian ornaments represented spirituality, royal identity, prosperity, and social status. Ancient artisans carefully crafted necklaces, crowns, bracelets, earrings, and temple jewelry.

GEMSTONE CRAFTSMANSHIP

Maṇi Bhūmikā Karma

Maṇi Bhūmikā Karma is associated with gemstone arrangement, luxury ornament creation, and artistic jewelry craftsmanship. This Kalā required deep knowledge of precious stones and metals.

Ancient Indian artisans used diamonds, pearls, rubies, emeralds, gold, and silver to create ornaments for royalty, temples, and ceremonial traditions.

DECORATIVE SKETCHING

Citrayoga / Alekhyam

Citrayoga and Alekhyam are classical arts related to drawing, decorative illustration, sketching, and artistic visualization. These arts were important in jewelry pattern creation.

Artists created floral motifs, geometric structures, decorative layouts, and symbolic patterns before crafting ornaments and jewelry.

METAL ENGRAVING ART

Takṣaṇa (तक्षण)

Takṣaṇa refers to the art of carving, engraving, shaping, and decorative metal craftsmanship. This Kalā played a major role in jewelry production.

Ancient artisans engraved floral, symbolic, and religious patterns onto gold, silver, bronze, and copper surfaces.

GEMSTONE EXAMINATION

Rūpya-Ratna Parīkṣā

Rūpya-Ratna Parīkṣā refers to the traditional knowledge of examining diamonds, silver, gemstones, and precious materials.

Experts evaluated gemstones based on clarity, shape, brilliance, reflective quality, and authenticity in ancient Indian ornament traditions.
