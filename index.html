64 KALĀS OF ANCIENT INDIA

The 64 Kalās were traditional Indian arts and skills mentioned in ancient Indian literature. These Kalās represented creativity, intelligence, craftsmanship, culture, and artistic excellence in Ancient India.

They included music, dance, painting, architecture, storytelling, decorative arts, ornament creation, textile arts, sculpture, and metal craftsmanship.

Ābharaṇa Kalā is one among these classical artistic traditions, focusing on jewelry design, gemstone arrangement, ornamental decoration, and artistic craftsmanship.

Ābharaṇa Yojanam (आभरण योजनम्)

Art of Ornament Planning & Jewelry Arrangement

Ābharaṇa Yojanam refers to the ancient Indian art of designing, planning, and arranging ornaments and jewelry. This Kalā focused on elegance, artistic balance, symmetry, and decorative beauty.

Traditional Indian ornaments represented spirituality, royal identity, prosperity, devotion, and social status. Ancient artisans carefully designed necklaces, bracelets, crowns, anklets, earrings, and temple ornaments.

This Kalā played an important role in preserving India’s rich jewelry heritage and artistic ornament traditions.

Maṇi Bhūmikā Karma

Gemstone Arrangement & Precious Jewelry Craftsmanship

Maṇi Bhūmikā Karma is associated with gemstone arrangement, luxury ornament creation, and artistic jewelry craftsmanship. This Kalā required deep knowledge of precious stones, metals, and decorative settings.

Ancient Indian artisans used diamonds, pearls, rubies, emeralds, gold, and silver to create ornaments for temples, royal families, and ceremonial purposes.

The Kalā demonstrates the combination of artistic creativity, technical precision, and craftsmanship excellence.

Citrayoga / Alekhyam (चित्रयोग / आलेख्यम्)

Decorative Sketching & Artistic Visualization

Citrayoga and Alekhyam are classical arts related to drawing, decorative illustration, sketching, and artistic planning. These arts were important in jewelry design and ornament visualization.

Artists created detailed sketches before crafting jewelry. Floral motifs, geometric patterns, symbolic forms, and decorative temple designs were commonly used.

This Kalā helped transform creative imagination into detailed ornament craftsmanship and artistic expression.

Takṣaṇa (तक्षण)

Metal Engraving & Decorative Craftsmanship

Takṣaṇa refers to the art of carving, engraving, shaping, and decorative metal craftsmanship. This Kalā played a major role in jewelry production and ornamental metalwork.

Ancient artisans used engraving tools to create detailed floral, symbolic, and religious patterns on gold, silver, bronze, and copper surfaces.

Takṣaṇa reflected precision, patience, technical mastery, and artistic excellence.

Rūpya-Ratna Parīkṣā

Gemstone & Precious Metal Examination

Rūpya-Ratna Parīkṣā refers to the traditional knowledge of examining diamonds, silver, gemstones, and precious materials.

Experts evaluated gemstones based on clarity, shape, hardness, brilliance, reflective quality, and authenticity. This knowledge was valuable in trade, royal courts, and ornament craftsmanship.

The Kalā combined scientific observation, traditional expertise, and artistic appreciation.
